After months of development, we are proud to present AxionOS 2.5. Our biggest release yet! Lots of stuffs changed, and we are excited to share them with you.

## Your Phone, Your Desktop

**AxPcMode** brings secondary display and desktop mode support to AxionOS. Your phone becomes the mouse and trackpad while projecting to an external display (via usb-c to hdmi or scrcpy new display option). 

No displayport support? We got you! with AxPcMode mode you can:
* Turn off main display while projecting the main display to scrcpy or another display
* Change resolutions on the fly during PC mode

During PC mode, your phone becomes immersive, with no status bar or navigation bar, just pure productivity.

## We're going Expressive!

The entire Axion UI has been redesigned with **Material 3 Expressive** and a new dual tone theme. We went through every component to make sure the new design language feels cohesive across the system instead of pieces from different projects stitched together.

* Material 3 Expressive integration across the Axion UI
* Dual tone theme support
* New Essential Notifications with M3E UI
* New M3E USB BottomSheet UI

## Launcher redefined

Pulse Launcher received its biggest update yet, bringing Material Expressive to the app drawer alongside a proper Pixel Launcher QSB/hotseat port and new organizational features.

* **Lock Recents App:** Prevent accidentally swiping away pinned tasks
* **Smart Drawer Folders:** Customizable folder grouping in the app drawer
* **New Folder Icons:** Folder icon support in app grid
* **New Private Space UI:** Redesigned for better usability
* **QSB & Hotseat:** Fixed and accurate port from Pixel Launcher
* **New App Drawer UI:** Material Expressive design

### AxBravia Engine

A port of Sony's Bravia Engine utilizing patterns observed from our research on Sony's implementation. Better display processing and color accuracy without the usual battery tradeoff and blackening of display.

### AxionFx Audio Engine

A new audio opensource processing engine with fine grained control over audio processing with M3E and friendly user interface.

### AxWallpaperEffects

New opensource wallpaper effects that brings life to your wallpaper. *(ctto: Saad Ullah Khan)*

## AxDynamic Bar

Shows important events like music/now playing and sports scores at a glance. Instead of pulling down the notification shade for everything, relevant information surfaces where you can see it.

## At a glance? QuickLook!

QuickLook has been improved to support smartspace events along with the smartspacer plugin and is now consistent across different UIs like clocks (ctto: Kieron Quinn).

## Lockscreen Refresh

New lockscreen clocks with improved customization options and a new **depth wallpaper effect**. Lockscreen widgets are now real widgets, not the limited implementation from before.

## *In omnia paratus!* — Ready for Anything, Again

AxBurstEngine returns with meaningful improvements derived from researched OEM strategies for smoother performance and efficient power handling.

* Smoother QS expansion over previous Axion versions
* Efficient background CPU handling ported from OxygenOS
* Revived **zlib-ng** and brought **jemalloc optimization** to Pixels *(ctto: GrapheneOS bionic allocator)*

## More Control, Less Hassle

### Custom ROM Hide

Blocks root and custom ROM detections from user apps at the system level. Banks and payment apps that invasively check partitions, mount points, and properties won't see what they're looking for.

### Do anything with your phone!

With Routines, you can automate almost anything on your phone without needing a third party app. Set up triggers and actions based on time, location, or device state.

### Your phone, your rules

Sandbox has been greatly improved thanks to additional privacy controls referenced from HMA-OSS. *(ctto: frknkrc44)*

### Typography matters

For the previous releases, we were finding a way on how to preserve or map the font weights correctly across the system. We noticed that some fonts like google-sans-flex were having wrong font weights. So, we checked whats wrong with our exisitng implementation and fixed it!

* Fixed font weight related issues
* Bypassed AOSP limitations on imported custom fonts
